🔭 Theodolite — Parts & Terms

Exp 3, 4, 5 · Most Asked

Q: What is a theodolite?
A precision instrument for measuring horizontal AND vertical angles. Transit theodolite telescope can be rotated 180° in a vertical plane (non-transit cannot).
Q: What are the 3 temporary adjustments?
① Setting up (centering over station with plumb bob) ② Leveling up (using foot screws & plate bubble) ③ Elimination of parallax (focus eyepiece then objective)
Q: What is transiting / plunging?
Rotating telescope 180° in vertical plane about the trunnion axis. Also called plunging or reversing.
Q: Repetition vs Reiteration method?
Repetition: Same angle added n times, final reading ÷ n. Eliminates graduation errors. Reiteration: Multiple angles measured from one station, horizon closed (sum = 360°).
Horizontal angle = Final reading ÷ No. of repetitions
Q: What is Face Left / Face Right?
Face Left: Vertical circle to the LEFT of observer. Face Right: Vertical circle to the RIGHT. Average of both eliminates instrumental errors.

📏 Levelling — Key Concepts

Exp 6 & 7 · Calculation Heavy

Q: What is levelling?
The art of determining relative heights (elevations) of points on Earth's surface. Uses a level instrument and levelling staff.
Q: Define BS, IS, FS
BS (Back Sight): First reading after setting up level (on BM). IS (Intermediate Sight): Any reading between BS and FS. FS (Fore Sight): Last reading before shifting level.
Q: HI Method formula?
HI = RL of BM + BS
RL of point = HI − IS or FS
Check: ΣBS − ΣFS = Last RL − First RL
Q: Rise & Fall Method?
Rise = prev reading > current reading
Fall = prev reading < current reading
RL = prev RL + Rise (or − Fall)
Check: ΣBS−ΣFS = ΣRise−ΣFall = Last RL − First RL
Q: What is a Change Point (CP)?
A stable point where both FS and BS are taken. Used when instrument is shifted. Must be on firm ground.

⚡ Total Station — All Functions

Exp 8, 9, 10 · Very Important

Q: What is a Total Station?
An instrument combining EDM (Electronic Distance Measurement) + Electronic Digital Theodolite (EDT). Measures distances, horizontal & vertical angles, coordinates simultaneously.
Q: Two methods of EDM distance measurement?
Phase Shift Method: Uses continuous EM waves; compares phase of outgoing vs reflected wave. Pulsed Laser Method: Measures time for laser pulse to travel to target and back. D = vt/2
2D = v × t (Pulsed laser method)
where v = speed of light, t = travel time
Q: What does REM function do?
Remote Elevation Measurement — measures height of inaccessible points (e.g. top of building) without placing prism at that point. Set prism at base, focus top.
Q: Key display symbols?
HR Horiz angle right HD Horiz distance VD Elevation diff SD Slope distance N/E/Z Coordinates

🌀 Curve Setting (Rankine's)

Exp 10 · Concept + Procedure

Q: What is a simple curve?
A circular arc in the horizontal plane with central angle < 180°. Defined by 3 points using radius, chord, and central angle.
Q: Right face vs Left face curve?
Right face curve: starts left, ends right of start point. Left face curve: starts right, ends left.
Q: What stakeout inputs are needed?
Either: Radius + Start point, OR Center coordinates, OR Chord length + Rise (mid-ordinate)
Arc length L = R × θ (θ in radians)
Chord = 2R sin(θ/2)
Mid-ordinate m = R(1 − cos θ/2)
Q: Procedure using Total Station?
1. Set up TS at P1. 2. Go to Stakeout → Arcs → Key In. 3. Enter method (Two points + radius). 4. Enter start point (BM8), end point (BM9), radius. 5. Press CALC → STORE. 6. Stakeout points along arc.

🛰️ GPS — Principles & Operation

Exp 11 · Conceptual Questions

Q: How does GPS work?
Trilateration — calculates position from distance to ≥4 satellites whose positions are known. 3 satellites for 2D position, 4th satellite to eliminate receiver clock error.
Q: What is trilateration?
Determining position from known distances (not angles) from reference points. Different from triangulation which uses angles.
Q: What is absolute vs differential GPS?
Absolute: Single receiver, ±10–20m accuracy. DGPS: Base station + rover, ±1m. RTK: Real-time kinematic, centimeter accuracy.
Q: Minimum satellites for accuracy?
Minimum 4 satellites for 3D position fix. Our GPS needs signals from 5 satellites for 3m circumference accuracy (per lab manual).
Q: How to record a point?
Power ON → wait for satellite lock (5+ satellites) → Go to Mark Waypoint → records Latitude, Longitude, Elevation.

🧭 Traverse & Compass Survey

Exp 2 & 5 · Angle Checks

Q: Open vs Closed traverse?
Closed traverse: ends at starting point. Used for wide areas, lakes, boundaries. Open traverse: doesn't close. Used for roads, railways, canals.
Q: Check for closed traverse angles?
Sum of interior angles = (2n − 4) × 90°
Sum of exterior angles = (2n + 4) × 90°
where n = number of sides
Q: FB and BB relationship?
BB = FB ± 180°
(+ if FB < 180°, − if FB > 180°)
Q: Included angle from compass?
Included angle = BB of previous line − FB of next line
Q: What is Gale's Traverse Table?
Standard tabular format for traverse computation: columns for station, line, length, bearing, quadrant, consecutive coordinates (Latitude N/S, Departure E/W), corrections, independent coordinates.

📐 Chain Survey & Instruments

Exp 1 · Basic Concepts

Q: Principle of chain surveying?
Only linear measurements are taken. Area divided into triangles (simplest figure plottable from 3 side lengths without knowing angles).
Q: Types of chains?
Metric 20m, 100 links Gunter's 66ft, 100 links Engineer's 100ft, 100 links Revenue 33ft, 16 links
Q: Types of offsets?
Perpendicular offsets: Right angle to chain line (more accurate). Oblique offsets / Tie lines: At other angles, used for distant points.
Q: What is ranging?
Establishing intermediate points on a survey line so all points are in alignment. Done by two surveyors using ranging rods and code of signals.
